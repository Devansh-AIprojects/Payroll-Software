Intelligence Layers — Priority Order
What Goes On Top

Sequenced by dependency. Each layer requires the one before it to be stable. Don't skip ahead.

A
Priority 1 · Bridge Layer · Post-MVP
Anomaly Detection
Build right after engine stabilises
Research signal: AI can automatically detect mismatches in payroll data — wrong wage payments, benefit computation errors, tax deduction anomalies. Organisations using AI-driven payroll software observed a 20% improvement in payroll accuracy.
  • Flag when a Skilled worker's gross deviates from historical average by more than a configured threshold
  • Detect duplicate attendance punches from the fingerprint device before they hit payroll
  • Catch tier-boundary edge cases — e.g. 23.5 days → tier 1, but OT pushes effective days to 24
  • Missing daily sync detection: no data from device after N hours = alert, not a silent gap
Plugs into: Payroll engine output → DNA Engine (first real domain feed from payroll)

D
Priority 2 · Post-MVP — Architectural Upgrade
Real-Time Attendance → Payroll Sync
Requires Phase 4 parser stability
Research signal: Continuous attendance sync feeds payroll calculations daily. Dynamic OT rules auto-adjust pay structures. Real-time payroll syncs with time, attendance, and finance systems — ensuring instant adjustments for overtime, variable pay, or tax updates.
  • Replace month-end batch with daily fingerprint log sync via Supabase Realtime
  • Owner and HR see live gross liability as month progresses — no surprise at month end
  • Device failure detection: no incoming data after N hours triggers an immediate alert
  • Dynamic OT re-calc: each day's punch re-runs OT logic; state stays live in DB
Plugs into: Phase 4 fingerprint parser → Supabase Realtime → live payroll state table
B
Priority 3 · Multi-Tenant v1
Predictive Cost Forecasting
Needs 3+ months of payroll history
Research signal: AI algorithms analyse historical payroll data to predict future costs, spot seasonal trends, and flag anomalies. Empowers finance teams to plan better and avoid over- or under-budgeting.
  • "Next month's payroll will be ~₹X based on current attendance trajectory" — pushed as alert
  • Cotton season = more shift OT = cost spike prediction surfaced ahead of time
  • Mill owners think in production cycles, not calendar months — this speaks their language
  • Seasonal workforce pattern detection across years as data accumulates
Plugs into: Reasoning Core → output as dashboard widget or WhatsApp alert
F
Priority 4 · Multi-Tenant v1 — High Value
Compliance Auto-Monitoring
Requires DNA Engine live
Research signal: By 2026, compliance will no longer be manual effort. Labour and tax regulations change almost daily. AI reads, interprets, and applies new rules automatically — keeping policies current everywhere the business operates.
  • EPF rate changes, minimum wage revisions — Maharashtra state notifications ingested automatically
  • System flags: "New min wage for Skilled from June 1 — your LC tier-1 rate is below threshold"
  • Owner approves config update → decision logged in DNA Engine audit trail regardless of action
  • Multi-tenant killer feature: each mill gets state-specific compliance alerts automatically
Plugs into: DNA Engine (rules layer) → Reasoning Core → alert output
C
Priority 5 · Multi-Tenant v2
Adaptive Tier Config Suggestions
Needs DNA Engine + accumulated decision history
Research signal: AI-powered systems are replacing static config tables with adaptive config. Can aggregate market rate data, spot trends, and surface actionable insights for more equitable and competitive compensation strategies.
  • DNA Engine surfaces: "RF tier-1 rate hasn't changed in 18 months, Maharashtra market rate is ₹X"
  • Suggest → human confirm loop — never auto-apply rate changes to payroll config without approval
  • Decision logged back into DNA Engine regardless of approve / reject — builds decision history
  • Future suggestions improve as the engine learns from past owner decisions over time
Plugs into: DNA Engine (decision history) → Reasoning Core → owner action
E
Priority 6 · Multi-Tenant v2 — SaaS Pitch
Employee Self-Service
Not needed for STC Cotyarn (42 workers, owner knows everyone)
Research signal: AI chatbots answer employee queries on salary slips, tax declarations, and leave balances — 24/7. Reduces pressure on HR and improves employee satisfaction. Modern workforce expects digital access to pay information.
  • WhatsApp bot: "What's my salary this month?" → pulls from live payroll state in real-time
  • "How many days have I attended?" → from realtime attendance sync (Layer D dependency)
  • Not a priority for STC Cotyarn; critical for multi-tenant pitch at mills with 200+ workers
  • Dramatically reduces informal HR queries — mill supervisors spend hours answering these daily
Plugs into: Integration layer → WhatsApp Business API or web portal
G
Priority 7 · Multi-Tenant v3 — Early Mover
Earned Wage Access (EWA)
India EWA regulation still forming — build it clean now
Research signal: In 2026, waiting 30 days to access earned wages feels structurally outdated. EWA moves from perk to infrastructure. India regulation still forming — unlike the US where CFPB is classifying it as a credit product.
  • Worker attended 18 days → requests partial wage advance via WhatsApp or self-service portal
  • System calculates safe withdrawal limit → owner approves → logged as advance deduction next cycle
  • India EWA regulation still forming = early mover advantage if built clean before rules land
  • Strong differentiator for multi-tenant pitch to mills with blue-collar daily-wage workforce
Plugs into: Real-time payroll state → Integration layer (bank API) → deduction config

Key Insight
Layer A (Anomaly Detection) is the bridge between your traditional payroll system and the DNA Engine. It's the first real output the AI layer produces from payroll domain data — and maps directly to Step ① of the AI roadmap: DNA Engine + RAG for one business domain. Build it right after the payroll engine stabilises. The payslip rows are the first training ground.
